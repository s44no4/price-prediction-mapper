Info

Controls

  • Drag the Current and Strike horizontal lines on the Price Distribution chart to set the underlying price and the binary strike.
  • Drag the Current and Strike horizontal lines on the Price Distribution chart to update those prices directly.
  • Drag either +1 sigma or -1 sigma point on the Price Distribution chart to change Sigma. Both points control the same sigma value.
  • Use the Sigma slider for exact sigma values from 1 to 1000 bps.
  • Drag the T point on the top axis of the Prediction Distribution chart to set time remaining, and drag the trade cutoff hatch to stop new trades below that T.
  • Use skew_q, spread_q, min_edge_q, and max_edge_q to control probability-space bid/ask quoting.
  • Use Trade Imbalance to see how EV from 0 to 100 cents maps into Yes/No demand from -1 to 1, and drag its top-axis handle to tune multiplier or power.
  • Use the Exposure Cap formula dropdown to choose how max long and short exposure reduce toward expiry.
  • Use n_paths, n_steps, exposure_splits, and trades_per_step as the minimal simulation setup controls.
  • Click Run to simulate simple path PnL, draw the empirical PnL histogram, and update terminal risk metrics.
  • Use Long or Short above the Prediction Distribution chart to choose the active side.
  • Drag the VWAP line on the Prediction Distribution chart to update the displayed edge.
  • Use the mouse wheel over the left bps axis or right price axis of the Price Distribution chart to zoom the visible price range.
  • Drag the right price axis on the Price Distribution chart to pan the visible price range up or down.

Formulas and Mapping

  1. Prices are modeled in log-bps space: price = current * exp(bps / 10000).
  2. T is time remaining to resolution, so sigma_T = sigma * sqrt(T).
  3. The Price Distribution chart draws the selected density family over bps using sigma_T: Gaussian, Student-t, Laplacian, or Logistic. Non-Gaussian families are standardized so sigma remains the bps standard deviation.
  4. The current binary fair price is 100 * (1 - F(strike_bps / sigma_T)), where F is the selected distribution CDF.
  5. The Prediction Distribution samples possible future underlying prices over T, then maps each sampled price to a binary fair price using the same sigma_T.
  6. The resulting prediction distribution is plotted as probability mass per bin and normalized so the cumulative sum is 1.
  7. The green fair line on the Prediction Distribution chart is the mean of that plotted prediction distribution.
  8. Bid and ask are CDF quantiles skewed by position: center_q = 0.5 - position * skew_q, with spread_q around center and min_edge_q around fair.
  9. The simulation starts with position = 0 and cash = 0, updates quotes and exposure after every trade, and settles at 0 or 100.
  10. p95 drawdown is a terminal-loss proxy from final PnL, not path max drawdown.
  11. Trade Imbalance maps EV - 50 into a bounded demand signal using tanh, linear clamp, softsign, dead-zone, power, or their inverse curves.
  12. The Exposure Cap plot currently supports sqrt(T) and constant 1 / -1 caps over the full 1 to 0 time range.
  13. The header percentage is expected edge over VWAP: Long uses mean / VWAP - 1, Short uses (100 - mean) / (100 - VWAP) - 1.
